//A stone is falling into a large pool of water.
//Initially the stone is immersed in air.
//The stone has reached its terminal velocity in the air implying that drag (due to viscosity) balances the
//the pull of gravity.

//When the stone touches the water surface, waves form on the surface. These waves are mirrored by waves in the air.
//By analogy with golf and drops scripts we define SOs. The first is a discrete object and is all the air which is in motion due to the passage of the stone.
//The 2nd is also a discrete object and is all the water which is in motion due to the passage of the stone.
//The air and water are continua except for these 2 SOs. 
//The air SO grows as the stone approaches the water surface and starts to shrink when the stone is fully immersed in the water.

//Before the stone touches the water, the air and stone are a composite SO called stone_air and the water SO does not exist.
//When the stone touches the water the air SO, water SO and stone form a new composite object called stone_water_air. 
//The water SO is 0 size when it is created, it grows rapidly as the stone moves into and through the water. 
//Although the size is changing we consider it to be a single SO.

//When the stone is just fully immersed the air SO becomes a solitary SO and water and stone continue as a new composite SO called stone_water.
//If the stone comes to rest at the bottom of the pool the stone_water composite ends and the water and stones SOs are separate.
//The water SO begins to shrink as viscosity damps out the disturbances caused by passage of the stone.
//At a given instance the water is completly at rest and the water SO ends: All the water in the pool is again part of the continuum.
//We choose the moment the stone comes to rest to separate water and stone because there is no longer interaction between 
//the stone and the water.
